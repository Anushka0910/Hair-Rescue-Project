Male Pattern Baldness (Androgenic Alopecia)

  • Stage 1:

    • No Hair Loss In this stage, there is no noticeable hair loss or receding hairline.

  • Stage 2:

    • Minimal Hair Loss A slight recession of the hairline around the temples may occur, but it is generally not very noticeable.

  • Stage 3:

    • Receding Hairline The first signs of more significant hair loss appear with a noticeable recession of the hairline around the temples.

  • Stage 4:

    • Vertex Receding Hairline and Crown Thinning In addition to a receding hairline, there may be thinning or loss of hair at the vertex (crown) of the head.

  • Stage 5:

    • Further Receding Hairline and Crown Thinning The hair loss becomes more pronounced, and the receding hairline and crown thinning are more apparent.

  • Stage 6:

    • Larger Balding Areas The areas affected by hair loss are more extensive, and there may be less hair overall on the top of the head.

  • Stage 7:

    • Balding Bridge and Temporal Areas The bridge of hair between the remaining hair on the sides of the scalp and the crown becomes thinner, and the temporal areas may also experience significant hair loss.
